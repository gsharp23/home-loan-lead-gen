# FHA Loan (Federal Housing Administration)

## Overview
FHA loans are mortgages insured by the Federal Housing Administration, designed
to help lower- and moderate-income borrowers and first-time buyers who may not
qualify for conventional financing. The FHA does not lend money directly; it
insures loans made by FHA-approved lenders, which lets those lenders offer more
flexible terms.

## Who it's for
- First-time and repeat homebuyers with limited down-payment savings.
- Borrowers with fair credit (often FICO 580+).
- Buyers of primary residences (owner-occupied) — not investment properties.

## Key terms (2024)
- **Minimum down payment:** 3.5% with a credit score of 580 or higher; 10% for
  scores between 500 and 579.
- **Minimum credit score:** 500 (with 10% down); 580 for the 3.5% down option.
- **Mortgage insurance:** Upfront MIP of 1.75% of the loan amount, plus an annual
  MIP (typically 0.15%–0.75%) paid monthly. Annual MIP often lasts the life of
  the loan when the down payment is under 10%.
- **Loan limits:** Vary by county. In 2024 the "floor" is $498,257 and the
  "ceiling" in high-cost areas is $1,149,825 for a single-family home.
- **Debt-to-income (DTI):** Generally up to 43%, with exceptions up to ~50% with
  compensating factors (strong reserves, residual income).

## Property requirements
- Must be the borrower's primary residence.
- Must pass an FHA appraisal confirming the property meets minimum safety,
  security, and soundness standards.
- 1–4 unit properties are eligible if the borrower occupies one unit.

## Pros
- Low down payment and lenient credit requirements.
- Down payment can come from gift funds.
- Assumable by a future qualified buyer.

## Cons
- Mortgage insurance is often required for the life of the loan.
- Loan limits can be restrictive in high-cost markets.
- Property must meet FHA condition standards.

## Good-fit signals
A lead is a strong FHA candidate when they have a moderate credit score
(580–680), limited savings for a down payment, are buying a primary residence,
and have a manageable but not pristine debt profile.
