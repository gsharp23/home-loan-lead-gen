# USDA Loan (USDA Rural Development Guaranteed Housing)

## Overview
USDA loans are mortgages backed by the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Rural
Development program. They promote homeownership in eligible rural and suburban
areas by offering **zero down payment** financing to low- and moderate-income
households. The most common version is the Section 502 Guaranteed Loan.

## Who it's for
- Buyers in USDA-eligible rural and many suburban areas.
- Low- to moderate-income households (income at or below 115% of the area median
  income for the county).
- Borrowers seeking a primary residence with little or no down-payment savings.

## Key terms (2024)
- **Down payment:** 0% required (100% financing).
- **Minimum credit score:** No hard USDA minimum, but most lenders look for
  640+ for streamlined processing; lower scores require manual underwriting.
- **Guarantee fee:** Upfront fee of 1.0% of the loan amount (financeable), plus
  an annual fee of 0.35% paid monthly.
- **Income limit:** Household income must not exceed 115% of the area median
  income (AMI) for the county, adjusted for household size.
- **Debt-to-income (DTI):** Typically 41%, with flexibility up to ~44%+ with
  compensating factors.

## Eligibility requirements
- **Location:** The property must be in a USDA-designated eligible area. Use the
  USDA property eligibility map to confirm the address/ZIP qualifies.
- **Occupancy:** Must be the borrower's primary residence.
- **Income:** Household income within the program's adjusted limits.

## Pros
- No down payment required.
- Competitive interest rates and low monthly mortgage insurance equivalent.
- Reduced fees compared to FHA in many cases.

## Cons
- Geographic restriction to eligible rural/suburban areas.
- Strict household income caps.
- Only for primary residences; no investment or vacation homes.

## Good-fit signals
A lead is a strong USDA candidate when the property ZIP falls in an eligible
rural/suburban area, the household income is at or below 115% of the area median
income, and the borrower has little saved for a down payment. ZIP codes in
smaller towns and the outskirts of metro areas are worth checking against the
USDA eligibility map.
