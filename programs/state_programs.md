# State & Local Down Payment Assistance (DPA) Programs

## Overview
State Housing Finance Agencies (HFAs) and many city/county programs offer
**down payment and closing-cost assistance** alongside affordable first
mortgages. These are typically layered on top of an FHA, USDA, VA, or
conventional loan and are aimed at first-time buyers and modest-income
households. Terms vary widely by state, so always confirm against the specific
agency's current guidelines.

## Common forms of assistance
- **Grants:** Funds that never have to be repaid (often 2%–5% of the purchase price).
- **Forgivable second loans:** A 0%-interest second mortgage forgiven over time
  (e.g., 5–10 years) if the borrower stays in the home.
- **Deferred-payment loans:** A second loan with no payments due until sale,
  refinance, or payoff of the first mortgage.
- **Mortgage Credit Certificates (MCCs):** A federal tax credit for a portion of
  annual mortgage interest, improving the borrower's effective qualifying income.

## Typical eligibility
- **First-time buyer:** Many programs require not having owned a home in the past
  three years (first-time-buyer definition).
- **Income limits:** Capped relative to area median income (AMI), commonly
  80%–140% of AMI depending on the program and county.
- **Purchase price limits:** Capped by county/area.
- **Homebuyer education:** Completion of an approved homebuyer education course
  is frequently required.
- **Credit score:** Often 620–640+ depending on the paired first mortgage.

## Representative examples
- **California (CalHFA):** MyHome Assistance and the Forgivable Equity Builder
  Loan provide deferred or forgivable down-payment help for first-time buyers.
- **Texas (TDHCA / TSAHC):** "My First Texas Home" and "Homes for Texas Heroes"
  pair affordable first mortgages with DPA grants or second liens.
- **New York (SONYMA):** Down Payment Assistance Loan (DPAL) offers a 0%-interest
  second mortgage forgiven over ten years.
- **Florida (Florida Housing):** The Florida Assist and FL HLP second mortgages
  provide down-payment and closing-cost assistance.

## Pros
- Reduces or eliminates the cash needed to close.
- Stacks with low-down-payment first mortgages (FHA/USDA/conventional).
- MCCs can improve qualifying ratios.

## Cons
- Funds are limited and can run out for the year.
- Income, price, and first-time-buyer restrictions.
- Education requirements and longer processing timelines.

## Good-fit signals
A lead is a strong DPA candidate when they are a first-time buyer, have income
within the program limits for their state/county, and are short on cash to close
even if their credit is acceptable. Pair DPA with FHA for low credit or with a
conventional loan for stronger credit. Always verify the borrower's state and
county against the relevant HFA's current program terms.
